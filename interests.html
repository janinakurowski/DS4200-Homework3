Projects and interests

This page holds a few themes that guide my learning and practice. I like work that blends public health with data stories that serve people. I try to write notes that are calm and clear. I want readers to feel welcome even if a topic is new. I am drawn to simple charts that show just what the reader needs to see. I also care about trustworthy sources and the way we present them. Good citations invite readers to check claims and form their own view. I believe the best writing is kind to the reader and precise about methods.

I keep a small area below where I can test simple visual ideas with JavaScript and D3. It is a sandbox for learning. The goal is not a perfect chart. The goal is to practice one small idea and to see how the code connects to what appears on the screen. I will update this over time. For now it is only a placeholder. If you are curious about the details or want to share a trick that helped you, please send a note through the contact section on the home page.

  • Public health questions focused on prevention and root causes
  • Clear, kind writing that respects the reader
  • Simple web pages that work on phones and laptops
  • Reproducible steps to help others repeat the work
  • Open conversation and shared learning
